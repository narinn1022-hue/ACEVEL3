Singapore

Highlight

Singapore is a prosperous island city-state in Southeast Asia, known for its strategic location, bustling port, and multicultural society. It has a high-income economy built on strong manufacturing and financial sectors, and has achieved high standards of living since becoming independent in 1965. The country is a global hub for trade and business and is composed of one main island and many smaller ones.

HSBC Rain Vortex & Shiseido Forest Valley

Description: The world's tallest indoor waterfall (HSBC Rain Vortex) surrounded by a beautiful multi-level indoor forest (Shiseido Forest Valley). The view is spectacular during the day and even more breathtaking at night with light and music shows.

Location: Jewel Changi Airport (connected to Terminals 1, 2, and 3).

Operating Hours: This area is generally accessible 24 hours a day.

Rating: 4,8 Stars

Butterfly Garden

Description: A lush garden with more than 40 species of butterflies, a cave waterfall, and various tropical plants. It is a peaceful place to relax.

Location: Transit Area, Terminal 3, Levels 2 and 3.

Operating Hours: Open 24 hours.

Rating: 4.5 stars.

Changi Experience Studio

Description: This studio presents an exhibition on the history and operations of Changi Airport through a variety of fun games and digital simulations. Suitable for all ages.

Location: Jewel Changi Airport.

Opening Hours: Approximately 11:00 AM–8:00 PM (it is recommended to check the official website for the latest operating hours).

Rating: 4.8 stars.

Philippines

Highlight

The Philippines is a Southeast Asian archipelagic country in the western Pacific Ocean, comprised of over 7,000 islands divided into three major island groups: Luzon, Visayas, and Mindanao. It is a republic with a mixed economy, a diverse culture shaped by indigenous, Spanish, and American influences, and a population of over 112 million. The capital is Manila.

Rizal Park

Description: A 58-hectare park dedicated to José Rizal, the national hero. The park offers green open spaces for relaxation, walking trails, beautiful gardens, and soothing fountains. It is the perfect choice for spending time outdoors.

Location: Ermita, Manila (near Fort Santiago).

Operating Hours: Generally 5:00 a.m.–8:00 p.m.

Rating: 4.5 stars.

Fort Santiago (Intramuros)

Description: A historic defensive fortress within the walled city of Intramuros. The fortress houses a museum commemorating Philippine national hero José Rizal, who was imprisoned here before his execution. Intramuros as a whole (including Casa Manila and San Agustin Church) is a destination rich in history and can be explored by tricycle or on foot.

Location: Intramuros, Manila (about a 30-45 minute drive from NAIA, depending on traffic).

Operating Hours: 8:00 a.m.–11:00 p.m.

Rating: 4.5 stars.

SM Mall of Asia (MOA)

Description: SM Mall of Asia is one of the largest shopping malls in the world, offering almost everything from boutiques to electronics stores. The area also features an ice rink and an IMAX theater.

Location: Pasay City (in the Bay City area).

Opening Hours: 10:00 a.m. to 10:00 p.m.

Rating: 4.5–4.6 stars.

Thailand

Highlight

Thailand is a constitutional monarchy in Southeast Asia with a capital in Bangkok, known for its vibrant culture, delicious food, and stunning landscapes. It is bordered by Myanmar, Laos, Cambodia, and Malaysia, and has a diverse geography ranging from mountains to beaches. Officially named the Kingdom of Thailand, it is the only Southeast Asian nation that was never colonized.

The Erawan Museum

Description: The Erawan Museum is famous for its giant three-headed bronze elephant statue, placed atop a charming museum building. The museum has three floors representing the three realms of the universe, each displaying beautiful Thai cultural and religious artifacts. Its fantastic architecture makes it a very photogenic place.

Location: Samut Prakan (about a 30-45 minute drive from the airport).

Operating Hours: 9:00 a.m.–6:00 p.m.

Rating: 4.5 stars.

The Ancient City (Muang Boran)

Description: Known as the world's largest open-air museum, Muang Boran contains replicas of Thailand's most famous historical buildings, temples, and monuments, either in their original size or scaled down. It's an amazing way to see various Thai landmarks in one place.

Location: About 30-45 minutes drive from Suvarnabhumi Airport.

Operating Hours: 09:00–19:00.

Rating: 4.5 stars.

Lat Krabang Park (Suan Phra Nakhon)

Description: An ideal city park for walking, jogging, or simply enjoying the fresh air. This place is perfect if you just want to get away from the airport atmosphere for a while and do some light activities.

Location: Lat Krabang area, quite close to the airport.

Opening Hours: 5:00 AM–9:00 PM.

Rating: 4.4 stars.
